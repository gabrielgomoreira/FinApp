Binomial Pricing model and Black Scholes

Impacts of increasing binomial periods (n)

As you increase the number of binomial periods (n) the price begins to converge into the Black Scholes price. This would be similar to estimating the area under a curve using rectangular slices, as the amount of slices approaches infinity (taking the limit), you area estimation converges to the true value.

Hencemore, as you increase the number of binomial periods (n) the binomial tree branches begin to rejoin (except in case of a discrete dividend). The more possible paths that one can take to reach a certain leaf (the stock price at expiration, S_t) the higher the likelihood that this will be the stock price at expiration.
